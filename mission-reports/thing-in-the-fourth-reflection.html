📜 Iron Meridian - Mission Report

Mission:

🪞 The Thing in the Fourth Reflection

Squad:

  • Nyxara
  • Vexara
  • ObsidianIvy
  • Vokkar

Summary:

Dispatched to the abandoned Verge Edge Glassworks, the squad investigated reports of reflections behaving independently. What followed was a mixture of sharp deduction, questionable logic, experimental rock-throwing, and a truly heroic commitment to solving puzzles that did not yet exist.

Through exploration of warped reflection pools, hidden workshops, and unstable mirror arrays, the team uncovered fragments of a larger anomaly. They successfully navigated environmental hazards, defeated constructs and a rogue Akata, and assembled the components needed to trigger the core phenomenon.

However, while the anomaly itself was disrupted, the entity behind it—a shadow mage manipulating reflective space—escaped during the final confrontation after an explosive ceiling collapse.

Highlights:

  • Premature Genius: The squad attempted to solve multiple puzzles before encountering them. Confidence remained high throughout.
  • Shardling Distraction: A swarm of shardlings interrupted early overthinking and were efficiently dispatched.
  • Glass Rat Incident: Vokkar attempted to capture a glass rat, accidentally killed it, then dissected it, discovering it contained red glass rather than treasure.
  • Forge Experiments: The team tested all reflective pools using rocks, confirming fire, portal, and illusion properties. ObsidianIvy nearly committed to the grief pool before reconsidering.
  • Portal Commitment: ObsidianIvy entered a portal first while flipping the squad off. Vokkar followed with a cautious “head-first” strategy. The rest followed reluctantly.
  • Workshop Success: The squad solved the tool-slot puzzle (and accidentally solved it completely), unlocking all doors and securing the Sun Lens and additional loot.
  • Improvised Engineering: ObsidianIvy constructed a double-lens system, powered by a literal held fireball, successfully activating a hidden guardian chamber.
  • Darkness Solution: The final shard puzzle was solved by removing all light sources and plunging the room into darkness—revealing the hidden shard.
  • Akata Encounter: The creature tracked the party due to their weapons, resulting in a violent confrontation. Kobold summons were immediately (and accidentally) destroyed by allied spellcasting.
  • Execution Finish: Vokkar decapitated the Akata in a decisive final strike.
  • Mirror Ritual Chaos: The party summoned and subdued multiple false entities before revealing the true threat—a shadow mage.
  • Roof Tactics: Vexara and Vokkar climbed to the roof and deployed explosives from a previous mission, collapsing the ceiling mid-fight.

Outcome:

The reflection anomaly has been successfully disrupted, and the glassworks has stabilized. However, the controlling entity escaped during the final engagement.

  • ✅ Reflection phenomenon neutralized
  • ⚠️ Shadow mage escaped
  • ⚠️ Potential recurring threat

Reward:

  • 💰 Standard mission payout (1,500 gp)
  • 🧰 Assorted workshop loot and magical items
  • 🧪 Akata biological components (weapon upgrade potential)

Next Steps:

Investigate the identity and motives of the escaped shadow mage. Review recovered notes and mirror shards for signs of wider reflective anomalies. Also determine whether explosives should remain the squad’s default problem-solving tool.

Guildmaster’s Comment:

“You broke the mirrors, scared off whatever was pulling the strings, and somehow didn’t kill each other in the process. That counts as success… mostly. Next time, try solving the puzzle in front of you before solving three imaginary ones first. And for the record, ‘blow the roof off’ is not a sanctioned investigative technique. Effective, though.”
